How to cut 5mm acrylic cleanly

A structured workflow designed for SEO and AI visibility: steps, parameter ranges, FAQs, and internal links to use cases and troubleshooting.

Outcome

You will produce a clean, repeatable straight cut on 5mm acrylic — with less melting, less smoke staining, and fewer failed sheets.

Steps

  1. Keep acrylic flat on the honeycomb bed. Remove protective film only when needed (film can protect surfaces from smoke).
  2. Set focus precisely. Inconsistent focus is a top cause of cloudy edges.
  3. Run a low-power outline to confirm position and jig alignment.
  4. Enable air assist and keep airflow stable. Too much turbulence can worsen edge haze.
  5. Compare single vs multi-pass using a small array test. Document results.
  6. Save a settings card: power/speed/passes/air/focus/masking/notes.
Tip: Put the final settings card on the material page (SSOT) and link to it from use cases and troubleshooting.

FAQ

Why is my acrylic edge cloudy?

Common causes: focus offset, unstable air assist, protective film residue, or too many low-power passes that heat-soak the edge.

Why does acrylic melt and re-fuse?

Heat accumulation. Try faster speed with adequate power, ensure airflow, and improve exhaust.

Why are there burn marks?

Smoke staining can be reduced by air assist, masking, and cleaner exhaust flow.
