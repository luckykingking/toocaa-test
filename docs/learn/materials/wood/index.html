Wood Laser Cutting Guide

Everything you need to laser cut and engrave wood at home or in the studio — best wood species, plywood and MDF settings, kerf control, safe-to-cut materials, project ideas, and FAQs.

If you are new to laser cutting wood, start with the recommendations below. Diode lasers cut and engrave most softwoods, plywoods and MDF, but a small amount of prep — and the right power, speed, and pass count — saves time, char, and ruined material. The settings here target a TOOCAA L2 or NOVA class desktop laser; adjust for your specific machine.

1

Recommended wood for laser cutting

Helps you pick a wood or board that cuts cleanly on a desktop laser.

Basswood / Maple

Low resin, smooth grain, light color — the easiest wood to cut and engrave.

Birch Plywood

Even layers, glue with low char. Best general-purpose laser plywood.

MDF Board

Cheap, uniform density, great for engraving. Cuts cleanly with strong ventilation.

Walnut

Deep, rich tone. Ideal for keepsakes and signage; needs slower passes.

Poplar Plywood

Light and inexpensive — common big-box ply. Watch for inner voids.

Cherry

Reddish hardwood with high contrast engraving. Avoid pieces with sap pockets.

4

Safe to cut, cut with care, do not cut

Quick safety reference before you press start.

✓Safe to laser cut
  • Solid hardwoods & softwoods
  • Birch, poplar & maple plywood
  • MDF (with ventilation)
  • Cork, cardboard, balsa
!Cut with care
  • Resinous pine — flare risk
  • Boards with knots or voids
  • Treated or stained wood
  • Test new sheet glue first
✕Do NOT laser cut
  • PVC / vinyl — toxic chlorine gas
  • ABS plastic — cyanide fumes
  • Polycarbonate (Lexan)
  • Fiberglass / carbon fiber
iSafety checklist
  • Goggles for the laser wavelength
  • Air assist + fume extractor
  • Fire extinguisher within reach
  • Never leave the machine unattended

7

Frequently asked questions

Answers to the most common laser-cutting-wood questions.

?What is the best wood for laser cutting?
Basswood, birch plywood, poplar, and MDF are the easiest woods to laser cut. They have low resin, even density, and a light tone that minimizes visible char. Walnut and cherry cut beautifully but burn faster — slow your speed or add an extra pass.
?Can a diode laser cut wood?
Yes. A 10W diode laser can cut up to 5–6 mm basswood or birch plywood in 1–3 passes. A 20W diode laser handles 8–10 mm wood and most 3 mm hardwoods in a single pass with air assist enabled.
?How do I avoid burnt edges and smoke stains on wood?
Apply masking tape to the surface before cutting, turn on air assist, drop power and add a pass instead of pushing harder, and choose low-resin woods. After cutting, wipe smoke with a damp cloth or 320-grit sandpaper.
?How thick of wood can a laser cut?
Cut depth depends on laser power. A 10W diode reaches about 6 mm wood; a 20W diode 8–10 mm with multi-pass; a 40W+ CO2 laser cuts 15 mm and beyond cleanly in one pass. Hardwoods cut slightly slower than softwoods at the same setting.
?Is it safe to laser cut MDF?
MDF cuts cleanly but its resin binder produces formaldehyde-bearing smoke. Always run an enclosed machine with strong ventilation or a dedicated fume extractor. Do not cut MDF in an unventilated room.
?Why is my laser not cutting all the way through plywood?
Most often the inner glue layer is denser than the outer veneer. Lower the speed by 20%, add a pass, confirm the focus, and check the board for warp — even a 1 mm gap can leave the bottom uncut.
