1) TL;DR

Engraving irregular shapes is the most common challenge for gift makers. The solution is a custom nest jig — a piece of MDF with a laser-cut pocket that matches your object's outline. This holds the piece in a known, repeatable position so your design lands exactly where intended. For curved surfaces like tumblers, limit the engraving area to the flattest zone (±5mm from apex) or use a rotary attachment for full wraps. This page covers positioning strategies for the most popular gift items.

3) HowTo: Engrave a personalized wooden ornament using a nest jig

This workflow works for any flat irregular shape: ornaments, pet tags, guitar picks, etc.

StepWhat you doWhy
1Trace the object outline: place it on a flatbed scanner or trace by hand, then digitize in TOOCAA Studio.An accurate outline is the foundation of a good nest jig.
2Create a nest jig: cut the outline as a pocket in 3mm MDF. Add registration crosshairs at two corners.The pocket holds the object flush and in a known XY position.
3Place the jig on the honeycomb bed. Drop the object into the pocket. Set focus on the object surface (not the jig).Focus must match the actual engrave surface, which may be higher than the jig.
4Align your design to the registration marks. Run a frame preview at low power to verify placement.Confirms the design lands on the object and not on the jig edge.
5Engrave using tested settings (e.g., wood ornament: 50% power / 5000 mm/min / 1 pass).Moderate settings prevent burning through thin ornament blanks.
6Swap the object, drop in the next one, and repeat — no realignment needed.The jig makes batch gift production fast and consistent.
Pro tip: For curved items like tumblers, create a V-block cradle from two angled MDF pieces. Engrave only the top-facing flat zone. For full wraps, consider a rotary attachment.

4) Common questions

Real questions from gift makers and personalization hobbyists.

Can I engrave a round tumbler with a flat-bed laser?

Yes, but only on a small flat or near-flat area. For full wrap-around engraving you need a rotary attachment. For flat-area engraving on round objects, use a V-block or cradle jig to stabilize the object and engrave the top-facing surface.

How do I position an oddly shaped object like a guitar pick?

Create a custom nest jig: laser-cut a pocket in 3mm MDF that matches the object's outline. The object drops in and is held in a known position. Use registration marks in your design file that align with the jig corners.

My engraving on curved surfaces looks distorted

Distortion happens because the focal distance changes across the curve. Limit your engraving area to the flattest portion of the curve (typically ±5mm from the apex). For larger engravings, use a rotary attachment.

What's the best gift material for beginners?

Flat wooden ornaments, bamboo coasters, and leather keychains are the most forgiving. They are flat (no positioning issues), engrave cleanly at moderate settings, and have high perceived value as gifts.
