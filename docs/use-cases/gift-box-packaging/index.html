Laser Cut Gift Boxes & Packaging

Design custom boxes, sleeves, inserts, tags, and packaging prototypes that make handmade products feel more premium and organized.

See project ideas Open settings calculator
Best material
Basswood, kraft board, MDF
Difficulty
Medium
Job types
Cut + Score + Engrave

Packaging content needs to help users think in folds, slots, inserts, and presentation. A laser cutter is useful for one-off gift boxes and small-batch brand packaging because there is no die-cut minimum. TOOCAA users can test a box template, adjust finger joints or bend lines, and produce short runs for seasonal products.

Quick answer

Start with a simple slip-lid box or sleeve. Use thin basswood for premium boxes, kraft board for prototypes, and engraved tags or inserts to connect the packaging to the product.

Frequently asked questions

Can I laser cut gift boxes?

Yes. Finger-joint wooden boxes, kraft sleeves, tags, inserts, and prototype packaging are all good laser projects.

What material is best for laser cut packaging?

Kraft board is best for prototypes and sleeves, while basswood or birch works well for premium gift boxes.

How do I score fold lines with a laser?

Use lower power and high speed so the laser marks the fold without cutting through. Always test on scrap first.

Can I make packaging for products I sell?

Yes. Laser cutting is excellent for short runs, seasonal packaging, and validating a package before mass production.
