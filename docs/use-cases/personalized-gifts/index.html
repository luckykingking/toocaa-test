Personalized Laser Engraved Gifts

Engrave names, dates, portraits, and messages on keychains, plaques, jewelry boxes, and wood blanks — one-of-a-kind gifts made in hours, not days.

See project ideas Open settings calculator
Best materials
Basswood, walnut
Difficulty
Easy
Primary job
Surface engrave

Personalized gifts are the highest-return use case for a diode laser. The material cost of a wood blank is low, the engraving time is short, and the perceived value is high because the item cannot be bought off a shelf. The main skill to develop is matching power and speed to the wood species so that the engraved text and image read clearly without filling in or burning too dark.

Quick answer

For text gifts, use walnut or basswood, engrave at 25–40% power and 3000+ mm/min, and keep font weight medium-bold at 12 pt or larger. For photo engraving, use a dithering preset, line interval 0.1 mm, on pale basswood.

Personalized gift project ideas

Six projects from quick keychain batches to premium walnut plaques. All are achievable on a 10W or 20W diode laser.

Custom Name Keychain

Engrave a name, date, or short message on 3 mm basswood blanks. Cut a rounded rectangle or custom shape outline. Add a keychain ring hole at the top before cutting.

Basswood 3 mm 15–30 min / batch Easy

Photo Memorial Plaque

Convert a photo to a dithered grayscale image and engrave on pale 3 mm basswood. Frame the engraved piece with a simple laser-cut border. A meaningful gift for anniversaries or remembrance.

Basswood 3 mm 60–120 min Medium

Wedding Anniversary Keepsake

Engrave the couple's names, wedding date, and a botanical border on a walnut or cherry plank. The dark wood gives a premium look that reads as high-end without complex finishing.

Walnut / Cherry 45–90 min Easy

Monogram Jewelry Box Lid

Engrave a monogram or floral pattern on a 3 mm basswood lid blank. Cut the lid outline and hinge slot. Assemble with a small brass hinge and stain the box body a contrasting color.

Basswood 3 mm 90–150 min Medium

Family Tree Wall Plaque

Engrave names and dates in a branching tree layout on a larger walnut or basswood panel. Use a vector illustration of a tree as the background and layer text over it.

Walnut / Basswood 60–120 min Easy

Personalized Luggage Tag

Cut a tag shape with a strap slot from 3 mm birch plywood, engrave name and contact details, and seal with a hard wax oil for moisture resistance. A durable, distinctive travel gift.

Birch plywood 3 mm 20–40 min / batch Easy

Recommended engraving settings

Engraving settings for common gift materials. Always run a 40 × 10 mm grayscale strip test before the final piece.

Starting parameters

Material & job Power Speed Passes
Basswood · Engrave · 10W 25–35% 2800 mm/min 1
Basswood · Engrave · 20W 28% 3200 mm/min 1
Walnut · Engrave · 20W 25–40% 3600 mm/min 1
Cherry · Engrave · 20W 28% 3200 mm/min 1
Basswood 3 mm · Cut · 10W 100% 180 mm/min 1–2

Photo engraving tip: Use a dithering algorithm (Floyd-Steinberg recommended) instead of plain grayscale. Set line interval to 0.10 mm and keep power under 30% on basswood. Run a 3-stop exposure test strip to confirm highlights, midtones, and shadows before the full piece.

Gift production workflow

From file prep to finished piece — a repeatable process that works for single pieces and batch runs.

01

Prepare the file

Separate engraving layers (text, image) from cut lines in your design software. Use a separate color layer for each so your laser software treats them independently.

02

Nest blanks efficiently

For batch gift runs, fit as many blanks as possible on a single sheet. Leave at least 3 mm between pieces to avoid burn bleed between adjacent pieces during engraving.

03

Run the engraving

Engrave all pieces on the sheet in one pass. Do not move the material between engraving and cutting. Keep air assist running to prevent smoke deposit on pale wood.

04

Cut the outlines

After engraving, switch to cut settings and run all outlines on the same sheet. Check that pieces are fully cut through before lifting; thin tabs hold better than partially cut edges.

05

Finish and package

Remove pieces, lightly buff with a soft cloth, and apply a thin coat of clear wax oil if the item will be handled frequently. Let dry before packaging or gifting.

Frequently asked questions

What is the best wood for laser engraved gifts?

Basswood is best for photo engraving and pale high-contrast results. Walnut and cherry are best for text gifts and premium-looking plaques. The dark grain gives a natural depth that looks expensive even with simple designs.

Can I engrave a photo on wood with a diode laser?

Yes. Convert the photo to grayscale and apply a dithering algorithm. Engrave at a fine line interval (0.1 mm or less) on pale basswood with low power and high speed. Run a multi-stop test strip to dial in contrast before the full image.

How do I make engraved text look sharp?

Use fonts with medium or bold weight at 12 pt or larger. Avoid very thin strokes at small sizes — they fill in from char. On dark woods like walnut, lower power often produces sharper text because it prevents heavy burning that bleeds into fine strokes.

How many keychains can I make from one sheet?

On a 400 × 400 mm cut area, 12–20 keychain blanks per 3 mm basswood sheet is typical, depending on shape. Batch engrave the full sheet, then cut all outlines in one pass for the most efficient production run.

How do I seal and protect a laser engraved gift?

A thin coat of clear wax oil or hard wax finish protects the wood and slightly darkens the engraved areas for better contrast. Apply with a cloth, let dry 30 minutes, and buff lightly. Avoid heavy lacquer on fine engraved text, as it can fill small lines.
