TOOCAA Use Cases

What can you make with a TOOCAA laser cutter?

From personalized gifts to QR code signs and photo engraving, a desktop laser cutter is most useful when the project, material, and settings match. Start with one of these real application scenarios, then test on your own material before making the final piece.

Explore use cases View wood settings
Cut and engrave real materials Wood, paper, leather, coated metals, and compatible acrylic colors are common laser projects. Clear or light acrylic may not cut well with a diode laser.
Always test first Settings are starting points, not guarantees. Run a small test before a full project, especially for plywood, MDF, photos, and QR codes.
Work safely Use ventilation and supervision. Do not laser cut PVC, vinyl, unknown plastics, pressure-treated wood, or materials that may release toxic fumes.

Before you start a project

These practical limits help customers get better results and avoid unsafe materials.

Material matters

Basswood, birch plywood, maple, walnut, kraft board, and some coated materials are common choices. Plywood quality can vary because of glue layers and voids.

Diode lasers need contrast

Photo engraving and QR codes work best on pale wood with clear contrast. Clear acrylic is usually not a good diode-laser cutting material.

Ventilation is required

Wood, MDF, paper, and leather create smoke. Use ventilation or a fume extractor and never leave the laser running unattended.

A test saves the final piece

Run a small sample using the same material, finish, focus, and air assist before cutting or engraving the full project.
