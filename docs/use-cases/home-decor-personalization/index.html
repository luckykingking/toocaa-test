1) TL;DR

Home decor is the sweet spot for laser engraving: high perceived value, low material cost, and infinite personalization options. The most popular projects are engraved cutting boards (family names, recipes), wall art (geometric patterns, silhouettes), and personalized signs (door signs, address plaques). Use quality hardwood like maple or walnut for the best contrast. This page covers the complete workflow from material selection to finishing, with tested settings and downloadable design templates.

3) HowTo: Engrave a personalized cutting board

The most popular home decor laser project — a beautiful result with straightforward settings.

StepWhat you doWhy
1Select a hardwood cutting board (maple, walnut, or bamboo). Wipe clean and let it dry completely.Hardwoods engrave cleanly with good contrast. Moisture causes uneven burning.
2Apply transfer tape or masking tape over the engraving area.Prevents smoke staining on the surrounding wood — peels off cleanly after.
3Import your design in TOOCAA Studio. Position using the camera preview. Add a quiet margin (≥15mm from edges).Margins keep the design visually balanced and away from board edges.
4Set focus on the board surface. Enable air assist.Correct focus + air assist = sharp lines and minimal smoke haze.
5Engrave: 70% power / 5000 mm/min / 1 pass for maple. Adjust for darker woods (walnut: try 55% power).Maple needs more power for visible contrast; walnut burns darker and needs less.
6Remove masking tape. Lightly sand any raised fibers with 320-grit sandpaper. Apply mineral oil or butcher block oil.Finishing enhances contrast and protects the wood. Mineral oil is food-safe.
Design tip: Bold fonts and solid fills engrave better than thin line art on cutting boards. Script fonts below 12pt may lose detail. Array Test CSV

4) Common questions

From home decor makers and personalization enthusiasts.

Is it food-safe to engrave a cutting board?

The engraving itself doesn't make the board unsafe, but engraved grooves can harbor bacteria. For decorative cutting boards (display only), this isn't a concern. For functional boards, engrave only one side and use the flat side for food prep, or apply food-safe mineral oil.

Can I engrave on painted or stained wood?

Yes — this produces excellent contrast. The laser removes the paint/stain to reveal lighter wood beneath. Test on a scrap first, as some paints produce more smoke. Ensure adequate ventilation.

How do I engrave a piece larger than the work area?

Use the passthrough slot (if available) or split your design into tiles. Engrave one section, reposition using registration marks, and engrave the next. TOOCAA Studio can help with alignment.

What finish should I apply after engraving wood decor?

For indoor decor, a light coat of polyurethane or Danish oil protects the engraving and enhances contrast. Avoid heavy coats that fill in fine detail. For items that will be handled, use wipe-on polyurethane.
