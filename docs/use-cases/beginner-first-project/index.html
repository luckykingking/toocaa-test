1) TL;DR

Your first project should be simple, repeatable, and guaranteed to succeed. We recommend engraving a wooden coaster using the included sample material and a pre-tested settings card. This removes the guesswork and builds confidence before you move to more complex materials. Total time from unboxing: ~30 minutes (including assembly).

3) HowTo: Engrave a wooden coaster — your first 30-minute project

Follow these steps exactly for a guaranteed-success first result. Use the included basswood sample and the settings below.

StepWhat you doWhy
1Assemble the machine following the Quick Start card. Connect power and USB/Wi-Fi.The L2 ships partially assembled — typically 10–15 minutes.
2Place the 3mm basswood sample flat on the bed. Use the focus block to set focal distance.Correct focus is the single most important factor for clean engraving.
3Open TOOCAA Studio. Import the included coaster design (or type your name in a text block).Starting with a simple design avoids software learning curve.
4Set engraving parameters: Power 60% · Speed 6000 mm/min · 1 pass.These settings produce a visible, clean engrave on 3mm basswood without burning.
5Run the frame preview (outline at low power) to confirm positioning.Prevents wasting material on misaligned jobs.
6Start the job. Stay nearby and observe the first 30 seconds.Verify the engrave depth looks correct; you can pause and adjust if needed.
7When complete, remove the piece and lightly sand if desired. Done!Your first finished piece — document the settings for future reference.

4) Common questions

Based on real first-time user support tickets.

What material should I use for my very first project?

Start with 3mm basswood or plywood. These are forgiving, affordable, and engrave cleanly at low power. Avoid acrylic or metal for your first attempt — they require more precise settings and air assist.

Do I need air assist for beginner projects?

Air assist is not required for light engraving on wood but is recommended. It reduces smoke marks and keeps the lens clean. You can add it later as you progress to cutting or working with acrylic.

How do I know if my laser focus is correct?

Use the fixed-focus block included with your TOOCAA machine. Place it on the material surface and lower the laser module until it rests on the block. Remove the block before starting. Incorrect focus is the #1 cause of poor results.

My engraving looks faint — what went wrong?

Most likely the power is too low or the speed is too high. Try increasing power by 5–10% or reducing speed by 20%. Also verify your focus distance is correct and the material surface is clean.
