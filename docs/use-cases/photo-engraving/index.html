Laser Photo Engraving on Wood

Turn portraits, pets, family memories, and special moments into engraved wood pieces with better contrast, cleaner highlights, and fewer over-burned shadows.

Job types
Photo engrave

Photo engraving is the most sensitive scene in this group because the final result depends on image prep, material color, line interval, and power control. A good page should teach users to test highlights, midtones, and shadows before committing to a full portrait. TOOCAA can produce strong photo engravings when the workflow treats the image as data, not just a picture.

Quick answer

Use pale, even-grain basswood or maple. Convert the image to grayscale, apply dithering, test 3-5 power levels, and keep the final engraving lower power than you think.

Photo Engraving workflow

A practical sequence users can follow from idea to finished piece.

01

Choose the right image

Use a bright, sharp image with clear subject separation and avoid muddy dark backgrounds.

02

Prepare the photo

Crop tightly, convert to grayscale, increase midtone contrast, and apply dithering.

03

Run a test strip

Engrave a small strip with several power levels to choose the best contrast.

04

Engrave the final piece

Keep the board flat and avoid moving it until engraving and outline cutting are complete.

05

Finish softly

Brush dust away and use a very light matte finish so fine image detail is not filled.

Frequently asked questions

What wood is best for laser photo engraving?

Pale, even-grain basswood or maple is best because it gives contrast without heavy grain interference.

Why does my laser photo engraving look too dark?

Power is too high, speed is too slow, or the photo has too much dark shadow. Lower power and adjust image levels before engraving.

Should I use grayscale or dithering?

Use dithering for wood photo engraving. It translates tones into dots that a laser can reproduce more cleanly.

Can I engrave pet photos on wood?

Yes. Choose a clear face photo, brighten the eyes, and test contrast before the final plaque.
