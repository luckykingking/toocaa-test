1) TL;DR

A laser engraver lets small brands create premium packaging without minimum order quantities. Engrave your logo directly on wooden gift boxes, kraft paper bags, leather hang tags, and acrylic display stands. The result looks high-end and is fully customizable (add customer names, order numbers, seasonal designs). Use an SVG version of your logo, create per-material settings cards, and build batch jigs for production efficiency. This page covers the full branding workflow from logo preparation to finished packaging.

3) HowTo: Engrave your brand logo on a wooden gift box

A step-by-step workflow that produces a professional branded box.

StepWhat you doWhy
1Prepare your logo as a high-contrast SVG (solid fills, no gradients). Convert text to outlines.SVG ensures clean scaling. Outlines prevent font substitution issues.
2Place the wooden box lid on the honeycomb bed. If the lid isn't flat, use shims or painter's tape to stabilize.Any wobble during engraving causes inconsistent depth and blurry edges.
3Set focus on the lid surface. Import the logo in TOOCAA Studio and position using camera preview.Camera preview shows exact placement — center the logo visually on the lid.
4Run a frame preview at low power to verify size and position.Catches alignment errors before committing — especially important on finished goods.
5Engrave at 60% power / 5000 mm/min / 1 pass for pine/basswood. Air assist on.Moderate settings produce a visible brand mark without deep charring.
6Inspect the result. If needed, wipe off smoke residue with a slightly damp cloth. Apply a light clear coat.A clear coat seals the engraving and gives a professional finish.

4) Common questions

From small brands, Etsy sellers, and makers building their packaging.

Can I laser engrave directly on kraft paper packaging?

Yes. Kraft paper engraves well at low power (15–25%) and high speed (8000+ mm/min). The result is a subtle, elegant darkening effect. Always test first — too much power will cut through. Keep a fire extinguisher nearby.

How do I maintain logo consistency across materials?

Create a separate settings card for each material. Use the same SVG source file and per-material profiles in TOOCAA Studio. Run a test piece from each material batch before production.

Laser vs. stamp for branding — which should I use?

Use laser for: detailed logos, variable text (names, serial numbers), and hard materials. Use stamps for: high-volume simple marks on soft materials (leather, clay, wax). Laser offers more flexibility; stamps are faster for simple marks.

Can I engrave on fabric tags or ribbon?

Natural fabrics (cotton, linen, canvas) can be engraved at very low power. Synthetic fabrics (polyester, nylon) melt and produce toxic fumes — avoid them. Always test on scrap and ensure ventilation.
