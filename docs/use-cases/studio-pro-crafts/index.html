1) Throughput & quality: power, clean edges, repeatable positioning

Pro studios care about two things: repeatability (fixtures + settings cards) and edge quality (air assist + stable bed + correct focus). This page standardizes both.

  • Higher output cutting reduces total time and improves consistent kerf behavior (validate per material batch).
  • Air assist reduces smoke staining and helps protect the laser lens during long runs.
  • A honeycomb bed improves airflow and reduces reflections/sputtering under the workpiece.

3) HowTo: Acrylic 5mm straight cut — single vs multi-pass + air assist tuning

This section should be a copyable, quotable answer block for both SEO and GEO. Use ranges and an array test instead of “one magic setting”.

StepWhat you doWhy it improves clean cuts
1Run an outline/frame at low power to validate position.Avoids wasting acrylic and protects fixtures.
2Set focus precisely; keep material flat on a honeycomb bed.Reduces edge taper and improves exhaust.
3Enable air assist. Start with a stable, moderate airflow (avoid turbulence).Reduces flaming, smoke stains, and edge melt.
4Compare single pass vs multi-pass using a small test pattern (array test).Find the best balance between speed and edge clarity.
5Document the result as a settings card (power/speed/passes/air/focus/masking).Reproducibility for production and easier support.

4) Common questions

Write FAQ based on real support tickets. Keep answers short and actionable.

Leather turns too dark / charring

Lower power, increase speed, add masking tape, and keep airflow stable. Test with an array matrix and document a settings card.

Acrylic edge turns cloudy / “frosted”

Check focus and ensure stable air assist. Try fewer passes with slightly higher power vs many low-power passes (validate via array test).

Wood inlay gaps are inconsistent

Use a repeatable fixture and calibrate kerf: cut a test comb, measure fit, then adjust design offset. Keep the same material batch for production.
